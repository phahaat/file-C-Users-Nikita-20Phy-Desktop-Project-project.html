METHODS OF CLASSIFICATION

  1. Hierarchical Classification: This method arranges items into a series of categories, with more general categories at the top and more specific categories nested below them, like a branching tree.
  2. Top-Down Classification: This is a way to assign items to increasingly specific categories within a hierarchical system. For instance, classifying a document might start with it being non-fiction, then academic, then history, then a specific historical period.
  3. Bottom-Up Classification: This involves grouping similar items together and then finding broader categories that encompass those groups. Imagine sorting a pile of clothes by color, then by type (shirts, pants, etc.)
  4. Statistical Classification: This method uses statistical techniques to classify items. Machine learning algorithms are a type of statistical classification, where a computer program learns to classify items based on a set of training data.
  5. Manual Classification: This refers to human experts classifying items based on their knowledge and experience. Librarians classifying books or art historians classifying paintings are examples of manual classification.
