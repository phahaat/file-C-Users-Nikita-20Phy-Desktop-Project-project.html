kingdom description example
animalia Multicellular eukaryotes that obtain nutrients by heterotrophy (eating other organisms). Lion, honey bee, human
plantae Multicellular eukaryotes that obtain nutrients by autotrophy (making their own food through photosynthesis). Oak tree, grass, algae
fungi Multicellular eukaryotes that obtain nutrients by decomposition or parasitism. They have cell walls made of chitin and are typically immobile Mushroom, mold, yeast
Archaea Unicellular prokaryotes that live in extreme environments. They have a different cell wall structure than bacteria and are thought to be one of the earliest forms of life on Earth. Halophiles (salt-loving archaea), thermophiles (heat-loving archaea)
bacteria Unicellular prokaryotes that are found everywhere on Earth. They can be autotrophic or heterotrophic and can be beneficial or harmful. Escherichia coli (E. coli), Staphylococcus aureus, Bacillus subtilis
